# Commonly Overlooked Deductions & Credits

Comprehensive reference for deductions and credits that taxpayers frequently miss.

## Above-the-Line Deductions (Reduce AGI)

### HSA Contributions
**Who misses it**: People who contribute outside of payroll
- Employer contributions show on W-2 Box 12 Code W
- Additional personal contributions are deductible
- 2024 limits: $4,150 (self), $8,300 (family), +$1,000 catch-up (55+)
- **Check**: Did you contribute directly to HSA outside payroll?

### Student Loan Interest
**Who misses it**: Parents paying for children's loans
- Up to $2,500 deduction
- Must be legally obligated OR paid for dependent
- Phaseout: $75k-$90k (single), $155k-$185k (MFJ)
- **Check**: 1098-E from lender, or reconstruct from statements

### Educator Expenses
**Who misses it**: Teachers who don't itemize
- $300 per educator ($600 if both spouses are educators)
- K-12 teachers, counselors, principals, aides
- Includes: Books, supplies, equipment, software, COVID protective items
- **Check**: Any classroom expenses paid out of pocket?

### Self-Employed Health Insurance
**Who misses it**: Self-employed with spouse's employer plan available
- 100% of premiums deductible
- Includes: Medical, dental, vision, long-term care (age-limited)
- Can claim even if spouse could cover you (if you don't enroll)
- **Check**: Schedule C filer paying own health insurance?

### IRA Contributions
**Who misses it**: People who contribute after year-end
- Can contribute until April 15 for prior year
- Traditional IRA may be deductible depending on income/plan coverage
- **Check**: Any IRA contributions between Jan 1 and April 15?

### Alimony Paid
**Who misses it**: People with older divorce agreements
- Only deductible for divorces finalized BEFORE January 1, 2019
- Must be cash payments per divorce decree
- **Check**: Divorce date and payment records

### Moving Expenses (Military Only)
**Who misses it**: Active duty military members
- Deductible for PCS moves
- Includes: Transportation, lodging, storage
- Not available to civilians since 2018
- **Check**: Active duty with PCS orders?

### Early Withdrawal Penalty
**Who misses it**: People who cashed CDs early
- Penalty shown on 1099-INT Box 2
- Fully deductible above-the-line
- **Check**: Review all 1099-INT forms for Box 2 amounts

### Jury Duty Pay Remitted to Employer
**Who misses it**: Employees whose company pays salary during jury duty
- Jury pay is taxable income
- If you gave it to employer (who paid your salary), deduct it
- **Check**: Did employer require you to turn over jury pay?

## Itemized Deductions

### Medical Expenses

#### Often Missed Medical Deductions
| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Medicare Part B/D premiums | Deducted from Social Security, often forgotten |
| Medicare Supplement premiums | Medigap policies |
| Long-term care insurance | Age-based limits apply |
| COBRA premiums | Often very high, frequently missed |
| ACA Marketplace premiums | If not claiming Premium Tax Credit |
| Dental implants, orthodontia | Major dental work |
| Hearing aids and batteries | Including exams |
| Prescription glasses/contacts | Including eye exams |
| Prescription medications | Including insulin, medical marijuana where legal |
| Medical equipment | Wheelchairs, walkers, oxygen |
| Home modifications | Ramps, grab bars, wider doorways (for medical necessity) |
| Transportation to medical care | 21 cents/mile (2024) plus parking and tolls |
| Lodging for medical care | Up to $50/night when away from home |
| Weight loss programs | If prescribed for specific disease (obesity, hypertension) |
| Smoking cessation programs | Including prescription aids |
| Fertility treatments | IVF, medications |
| Service animals | Purchase, training, food, vet bills |
| Special education | If recommended by doctor for learning disability |
| Therapy | Physical, occupational, speech, mental health |

#### Medical Expenses NOT Deductible
- Cosmetic surgery (unless for accident/disease)
- General health club dues
- Non-prescription medications (except insulin)
- Funeral expenses
- Most cosmetic dental work

### State and Local Taxes (SALT)

#### Maximize Within $10,000 Cap
| Tax Type | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Real estate taxes | All properties owned |
| Personal property tax | Vehicle registration (ad valorem portion only) |
| State income tax OR sales tax | Choose higher; sales tax better for no-income-tax states |
| Foreign taxes | Can deduct here OR take credit - compare |

#### Sales Tax Option
- Use IRS Sales Tax Calculator for table amount
- Add major purchases: Vehicles, boats, aircraft, home building materials
- Often better for: TX, FL, WA, NV, SD, WY, AK, TN, NH residents

### Mortgage Interest

#### Commonly Missed
| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Points on purchase | Deductible in year paid if itemizing |
| Points on refinance | Amortize over loan life, plus remainder when sold/refinanced |
| Mortgage insurance (PMI) | Check 1098 Box 5; income limits apply |
| Home equity loan interest | Only if used for home improvement |
| Second home interest | Up to combined $750k acquisition debt |

### Charitable Contributions

#### Cash Donations
| Situation | Documentation Required |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Under $250 | Bank record, receipt, or written communication |
| $250 or more | Written acknowledgment from charity |
| Payroll deduction | Pay stub or W-2 plus pledge card |

#### Non-Cash Donations
| Item | Documentation |
|------|--------------|
| Clothing/household items | Itemized list with values; must be "good condition or better" |
| Vehicle over $500 | Form 1098-C from charity; deduction usually limited to sale price |
| Stock/securities | FMV if held >1 year; basis if held <1 year |
| Over $500 total non-cash | Form 8283 required |
| Over $5,000 single item | Qualified appraisal required |

#### Often Overlooked Charitable
| Item | Rate/Notes |
|------|-----------|
| Mileage for charity work | 14 cents/mile |
| Out-of-pocket volunteer expenses | Supplies, uniforms, etc. |
| Convention travel for charity | If serving as delegate |
| Foster parent expenses | Unreimbursed costs beyond stipend |
| Student hosted by charity | $50/month if no compensation received |

### Casualty and Theft Losses

**Current rules (2018+):**
- Only for federally declared disasters
- $100 floor per event
- 10% of AGI floor
- Reduce by insurance reimbursement
- **Check FEMA declarations**: fema.gov/disasters

## Tax Credits

### Child Tax Credit
**Who misses it**: High earners who assume they're phased out
- $2,000 per child under 17
- Phaseout doesn't start until $200k (single) or $400k (MFJ)
- Credit for Other Dependents: $500 for older children, relatives
- **Check**: All qualifying children claimed?

### Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)
**Who misses it**: Middle-income families, workers without children
- Up to $7,830 (2024, 3+ children)
- Available even with 0 children (smaller credit, ages 25-64)
- Investment income must be under $11,600
- **Check**: Run EITC calculator even if you think income is too high

### Child and Dependent Care Credit
**Who misses it**: Parents using summer camps
- Qualifying expenses: Daycare, nanny, au pair, day camp, after-school programs
- NOT qualifying: Overnight camp, tutoring, food/clothing
- Expense limits: $3,000 (one child), $6,000 (two+)
- **Check**: Did you pay for any care while working?

### American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC)
**Who misses it**: Parents not tracking all expenses
- Up to $2,500 per student (first 4 years of college)
- Includes: Tuition, fees, books, supplies, equipment
- 40% refundable (up to $1,000)
- Phaseout: $80k-$90k (single), $160k-$180k (MFJ)
- **Check**: All education expenses counted, not just 1098-T?

### Lifetime Learning Credit
**Who misses it**: Part-time students, professional development
- Up to $2,000 per return
- Any year of college, graduate school, professional courses
- No degree program required
- **Check**: Any courses taken to improve job skills?

### Saver's Credit
**Who misses it**: Lower-income retirement savers
- 10%-50% of retirement contributions (max $2,000 contribution)
- Income limits (2024): $76,500 (MFJ), $57,375 (HoH), $38,250 (Single)
- **Check**: Did you contribute to 401k/IRA?

### Foreign Tax Credit
**Who misses it**: Mutual fund investors
- Foreign taxes paid shown on 1099-DIV Box 7
- Usually small amounts but frequently missed
- Can claim directly if under $300 ($600 MFJ) without Form 1116
- **Check**: Review all 1099-DIV for Box 7 amounts

### Residential Energy Credits

| Improvement | Credit |
|-------------|--------|
| Solar electric | 30% of cost |
| Solar water heating | 30% of cost |
| Geothermal heat pump | 30% of cost |

| Fuel cells | 30% of cost |

| Central A/C | 30% | $600 |
| Water heater | 30% | $600 |
| Heat pump | 30% | $2,000 |

### Electric Vehicle Credits

#### New EV Credit (up to $7,500)
- Must meet domestic content requirements
- Income limits: $150k (single), $300k (MFJ)
- Vehicle price cap: $55k cars, $80k trucks/SUVs
- Can transfer to dealer for immediate discount

#### Used EV Credit (up to $4,000)
- 30% of price, max $4,000
- Vehicle must be at least 2 years old
- Price cap: $25,000
- Income limits: $75k (single), $150k (MFJ)

### Adoption Credit
**Who misses it**: People with failed adoptions
- Up to $16,810 per child (2024)
- Includes: Adoption fees, attorney fees, court costs, travel
- Special needs adoption: Full credit regardless of expenses
- Carryforward 5 years if can't use
- **Check**: Any adoption expenses, even unsuccessful?

### Premium Tax Credit
**Who misses it**: People who didn't reconcile
- Must file Form 8962 to reconcile advance payments
- May get additional credit if income was lower than estimated
- May owe if income was higher
- **Check**: Received Form 1095-A from Marketplace?

## Self-Employment Deductions

### Home Office
**Who misses it**: People who fear audit
- Simplified method: $5/sq ft, max 300 sq ft = $1,500
- Regular method: Actual expenses prorated by square footage
- Must be regular AND exclusive use for business
- **Check**: Do you have dedicated workspace?

### Vehicle Expenses
**Who misses it**: People who don't track mileage
- Standard rate: 67 cents/mile (2024)
- Actual expense method: Gas, repairs, insurance, depreciation
- Must keep contemporaneous mileage log
- **Check**: Start tracking mileage now for next year

### Health Insurance
**Who misses it**: Self-employed with access to spouse's plan
- Can deduct 100% of premiums even if spouse has employer plan (if you don't enroll)
- Above-the-line deduction (not Schedule C)
- Limited to net self-employment income
- **Check**: Paying own health insurance?

### Retirement Contributions
**Who misses it**: People who think deadline is Dec 31
- SEP-IRA: Up to 25% of net SE income; can contribute until tax filing deadline
- Solo 401(k): Must establish by Dec 31, but can fund later
- SIMPLE IRA: Must establish by Oct 1
- **Check**: Room for retirement contribution?

### Professional Development
**Who misses it**: People who think only formal education counts
- Books and publications
- Online courses and webinars
- Professional conferences
- Coaching and consulting
- Certifications and licenses
- **Check**: Any learning expenses for your business?

### Business Insurance
**Who misses it**: People who don't separate business insurance
- Liability insurance
- Professional liability (E&O)
- Business property insurance
- Cyber insurance
- **Check**: Any insurance for business activities?

## State-Specific Deductions

### 529 Plan Contributions
**Who misses it**: People who don't check state rules
- Over 30 states offer state income tax deduction or credit
- Some allow deduction for contributions to ANY state's plan
- Some require contributing to home state plan
- **Check**: Your state's 529 deduction rules

### Property Tax Credits for Seniors/Disabled
**Who misses it**: Seniors and disabled homeowners
- Many states offer additional credits or rebates
- Often separate application required
- Income limits usually apply
- **Check**: State property tax relief programs

### Renter's Credit
**Who misses it**: Renters in certain states
- Available in: CA, CO, CT, HI, IA, IN, MA, MD, ME, MI, MN, MO, MT, NJ, ND, PA, RI, VT, WI
- Usually income-limited
- **Check**: State renter's credit availability

## Carryforward Items (Check Prior Returns)

### Must Track Annually
| Item | Carryforward Period |
|------|-------------------|
| Capital losses | Unlimited |
| Net Operating Losses (NOL) | Unlimited (80% limitation) |
| Charitable contributions | 5 years |
| Passive activity losses | Until disposition |
| Investment interest expense | Unlimited |
| AMT credit | Unlimited |
| Foreign tax credit | 10 years forward, 1 year back |
| General business credits | Varies by credit |
| Residential energy credits | Until used |
| Adoption credit | 5 years |

### How to Find Carryforwards
1. Review prior year return Schedule D (capital loss carryforward)
2. Review prior year Form 8582 (passive losses)
3. Review prior year Form 4952 (investment interest)
4. Review prior year Form 8801 (AMT credit)
5. Review prior year Schedule A (charitable carryover)
6. Ask preparer for carryforward worksheets

## Year-End Planning Opportunities

### Before December 31
- Maximize 401(k) contributions
- Make charitable donations (bunch if near threshold)
- Realize capital losses (watch wash sale rule)
- Prepay deductible expenses (property tax, medical, etc.)
- Purchase business equipment (Section 179)
- Make state estimated payment (deductible this year if itemizing)

### Before April 15
- Contribute to Traditional IRA (deductible if eligible)
- Contribute to HSA (if still HDHP eligible)
- Contribute to SEP-IRA (for self-employed)
- Make estimated tax payment for Q1

### Before Tax Filing Deadline (with extension)
- SEP-IRA contribution for prior year
- Solo 401(k) employer contribution
