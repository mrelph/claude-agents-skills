# Formations & Tactical Systems

This document covers the tactical formation library the lineup builder draws from: forward line structures, defense pair systems, special teams formations, line deployment, and player rotation guidelines. It is **team-agnostic** — apply it through the lens of your team's `team-strategy.md`.

> Throughout this file, ratings reference the default 1.00–3.00 scale (lower is better). Adjust to your team's actual scale as needed.

---

## Forward Line Structures

### Four-Line System

**When to use**:
- Most regular-season games
- Pool play in tournaments
- When you want to wear opponents down through depth
- When you have a full roster
- Against teams that roll only 2-3 lines

**Philosophy**: Keep everyone fresh, dominate the third period, develop bottom-six players.

**Typical structure**:

```
LINE 1 — Top / Offensive (35-40% ice time)
LW/C/RW: Highest-rated forwards (elite tier)
Focus: Top matchup line, all special teams

LINE 2 — Skilled / Two-Way (30-35% ice time)
LW/C/RW: Strong skilled tier (1.50-1.75)
Focus: Two-way reliability, PP2 and PK

LINE 3 — Grind / Energy (20-25% ice time)
LW/C/RW: Middle tier (1.75-2.00)
Focus: Energy, physicality, forecheck, defensive responsibility

LINE 4 — Depth / Development (10-15% ice time)
LW/C/RW: Depth + development tier
Focus: Sheltered minutes for development players, energy shifts
```

**Rotation**: 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → repeat. Shorter shifts on Line 4 (45-60 sec). Double-shift Line 1 in critical situations.

### Three-Line System

**When to use**:
- Close playoff games
- Against very strong opponents
- When key players are absent and depth thins
- Late in tight games when you need top-line minutes

**Philosophy**: Maximize ice time for top players; everyone still plays but skewed.

**Typical structure**:

```
LINE 1 — Elite (40-45% ice time)
LINE 2 — Skilled (35-40% ice time)
LINE 3 — Energy/Depth (20-25% ice time)
Spot duty: Remaining players (3-8 min each)
```

## Defense Pair Structures

### Three-Pair System (Standard)

**When to use**: Most games with full roster.

```
PAIR 1 — Top / Shutdown (40-45% ice time)
LD: Top dedicated D    RD: Top F/D versatile or strong D
Focus: vs opponent's top line, PK

PAIR 2 — Two-Way / Balanced (35-40% ice time)
LD: Strong dedicated D    RD: Strong F/D
Focus: All situations, can jump into rush

PAIR 3 — Development / Sheltered (20-25% ice time)
LD: Development-tier D    RD: Strong partner (always)
Focus: Sheltered minutes, offensive zone starts
```

**Rule**: Development defensemen ALWAYS have a strong, experienced partner. Never pair two weaker players together.

### Four-Pair System (Tournament Depth)

**When to use**: Multi-game weekends, pool play, when ice time distribution matters more than maxing top players.

```
PAIR 1: Top dedicated D + elite F/D
PAIR 2: Strong dedicated D + strong F/D
PAIR 3: F/D + F/D depth
PAIR 4: Development D + strong partner
```

Equal-ish shifts; Pair 4 slightly less.

### Two-Pair System (Emergency / Late-Game)

**When to use**: Multiple D absences, very close late-game situations, overtime.

```
PAIR 1: Best dedicated D + elite F/D
PAIR 2: Next-best dedicated D + strong F/D
```

Back-and-forth, 60-90 second shifts.

## Special Teams Formations

### Power Play Systems

#### Umbrella Formation (5v4)

```
         Net-front F
              |
              |
    Half-wall — Slot/QB — Half-wall

         Point D
```

**Roles**:
- **Slot/QB**: Setup man, quarterback, high-slot threat
- **Half-walls**: Shot threats, pass options
- **Net-front**: Screens, tips, rebounds
- **Point**: Blue-line shot, one-timer threat

**Keys**: Puck movement to tire PK, get net-front body in front, shoot from point for tips/rebounds.

#### Overload Formation (5v4)

```
         Net-front F
              |
              |
    Half-wall - Slot - Half-wall (same side overload)

         Point D
```

**Philosophy**: Shoot first, crash net, get pucks on goal. Simple, direct.

### Penalty Kill Systems

#### Box+1 Aggressive (5v4)

```
        F1 (pressure)
            ↑
            |
    F2 ---- D1 ---- D2
            |
           F3 (rover)
```

**Personnel**: F1 = best two-way forward (pressure). F2 = box forward. D1/D2 = top defensive pair.

**Keys**:
- F1 pressures puck carrier aggressively
- Box stays compact (~15 ft)
- Rotate F1 when tired (avoid overcommit)
- Block passing lanes
- Force turnovers, create shorthanded chances

#### Passive Box (Protect Lead / 5v3)

```
    F2 -------- F3
     |          |
    D1 -------- D2
```

**Keys**: Compact (~12 ft), protect slot, block shots, only safe clears.

## Line Deployment

### Offensive Zone Faceoffs
- **Best offensive players** (top two lines)
- Quick-strike mentality

### Defensive Zone Faceoffs
- **Best defensive players** (two-way forwards + top D pair)
- Win draw, clear zone, simple

### Neutral Zone Faceoffs
- Match opponent strength; fresh legs matter most

### Late Game — Protecting Lead (last 2 minutes)
- **Use**: Top two lines, top two defense pairs
- **Avoid**: Development players in critical situations

### Late Game — Need Goal (last 2 minutes)
- **Use**: Top line + best offensive D
- **Pull goalie**: 90s if down 1, 2 min if down 2
- **Extra attacker**: Most responsible skilled forward

### Overtime (3v3)
- **Shift 1**: Two best F/D players + top D
- **Shift 2**: Next two best skilled players + second D
- **Shifts**: 45-60 sec max, rotate fast

## Game Situation Adjustments

### When Losing (by 1-2)

| Period | Adjustment |
|--------|-----------|
| 1st | Stay calm, stick to system, don't abandon four-line yet |
| 2nd | Increase top-line minutes ~5%, decrease bottom-line ~5% |
| 3rd | Consider three-line collapse, double-shift top line, pull goalie at appropriate time |

### When Winning (by 1-2)

| Period | Adjustment |
|--------|-----------|
| 1st | Foot on gas, don't sit back |
| 2nd | Maintain structure, keep rolling lines |
| 3rd | Smart defensive play, four-line freshness, block shots; don't turtle |

### When Tied (Close Game)
- Trust depth, grind it out, stay disciplined
- Late 3rd: slight uptick on top-line minutes, defensive responsibility increases

### When Dominating (up 3+)
- Roll four lines evenly
- Give development players extra ice time
- Consider playing backup goalie

## Player Rotation Guidelines

### Forward Shift Length
| Line | Length |
|------|--------|
| 1 | 60-90 sec (extend in critical) |
| 2 | 60-90 sec |
| 3 | 60-75 sec |
| 4 | 45-60 sec (limit development exposure) |

### Defense Shift Length
| Pair | Length |
|------|--------|
| 1 | 60-90 sec |
| 2 | 60-90 sec |
| 3 | 45-60 sec (protect development D) |

### Line Matching
- With strong depth: don't obsess over matchups; trust the system
- Use last change at home to put your top line vs their weak line
- Against elite opponents: match top vs top; win middle/bottom on depth

## Special Deployment: F/D Versatile Players

Players rated as F/D (capable at both forward and defense) are strategic gold. Use them to:

**Move forward → defense** when:
- Defense injury / absence mid-game
- Need more defensive structure
- Protecting a lead

**Move defense → forward** when:
- Forward injury / absence mid-game
- Need more offensive firepower
- Chasing a goal

**Most flexible players**: Elite-tier F/D (1.00-1.50) — use first.
**Easy moves**: 1.50-2.00 F/D — solid at both.
**Emergency only**: F/D players who lean strongly to one position.

## Goalie Rotation

**Season-long philosophy**:
- Both goalies get meaningful starts
- Recent performance ≥ blanket rotation
- Tournaments: split pool play; hot goalie in playoffs
- Back-to-back days: alternate

**Game assignment factors**:
- Opponent style (skill-heavy vs grinding)
- Recent performance trend
- Back-to-back load management
- Communicate the start 24-48 hours ahead

## Practice Line Combinations

Use practice to experiment before games:
- Test elite F/D players at forward vs defense — which maximizes impact?
- Test chemistry within a tier (which 2.00s click together?)
- Practice emergency scenarios (only 4 D, only 10 F)
- Try different special teams formations

## Keys to Success

### Tactical Discipline
1. **Stay in system** — don't freelance
2. **Support each other** — five together beats five individuals
3. **Backchecking is mandatory** — defense starts with forwards
4. **Simple wins** — don't overcomplicate

### Line Chemistry Factors
- Complementary speed and skill profiles
- On-ice communication and work-rate matching
- Clear role understanding within the line
- Players who have succeeded together before

### Adjusting on the Fly
- Use F/D versatile players to solve problems
- Adjust within tiers (interchangeable players) before reshuffling everything
- Communicate changes clearly to players
- Trust the system — small adjustments first
