# Game Planning & Opponent Strategy

A systematic approach to preparing for games, scouting opponents, building lineups, and making in-game adjustments. Team-agnostic — apply through your team's `team-strategy.md`.

---

## Pre-Game Preparation Checklist

### 24-48 Hours Before Game

**1. Confirm Roster Availability**
- [ ] **Check TeamSnap RSVPs** (if TeamSnap MCP is connected) — pull RSVP statuses for the game event
- [ ] If TeamSnap unavailable, check for player absences manually (school, family, injuries)
- [ ] Categorize players: Available / Out / Uncertain (Maybe / No Response)
- [ ] Follow up on uncertain players
- [ ] Develop contingency lineup for absences
- [ ] Communicate with parents about availability

**2. Scout Opponent (If Possible)**
- [ ] Review previous games against this opponent
- [ ] Check league standings and recent results
- [ ] Ask other coaches about this team
- [ ] Identify their top players and style

**3. Select Starting Goalie**
- [ ] Consider opponent strength
- [ ] Review recent goalie performance
- [ ] Check back-to-back situations
- [ ] Communicate decision to both goalies

**4. Build Initial Lineup**
- [ ] Forward lines based on available players
- [ ] Defense pairs
- [ ] Special teams units
- [ ] Role assignment per player

**5. Communication**
- [ ] Email lineup to team
- [ ] Send game details (time, location, arrival time)
- [ ] Share special instructions
- [ ] Answer parent/player questions

### Day of Game

Arrival time (~90 minutes before puck drop):
- [ ] Players arrive and dress
- [ ] Brief team meeting (5-10 min)
- [ ] Warm-up (~30 min on ice)
- [ ] Final lineup adjustments if needed

## Opponent Scouting Framework

### Team Identity Questions
1. What's their style? (Skill-based? Physical? Defensive?)
2. How many lines do they roll? (2/3/4)
3. Strengths? (PP? Speed? Size?)
4. Weaknesses? (Defense? Goaltending? Depth?)

### Forwards
- Top line identification
- Scoring threats
- Physical players
- Speed / breakaway threats

### Defense
- How many D-capable players?
- Top pair / shutdown defenders
- Weak link to target?
- Skilled D who jump up?

### Goaltending
- Starter and tendencies
- Strengths (positioning / reflexes / puck handling)
- Weaknesses (high / low / rebounds)
- How do we beat them? (Traffic, five-hole, corners)

### Special Teams
- PP formation and threats
- PK approach (aggressive / passive)
- Discipline (do they take penalties?)

### Team Style
- Speed vs Skill vs Physical
- Forecheck intensity
- Defensive structure (collapse / aggressive)
- Line matching tendencies

## Step-by-Step Lineup Construction

### Step 1: Confirm Available Players

**If TeamSnap MCP is available**: Pull RSVPs for the game event first. Map each player to the roster, then fill in the lists below with confirmed Yes players. Treat Maybe / No Response as uncertain — build the base lineup without them but plan to slot them in if confirmed.

```
Available Forwards: ___________
Available Defense:  ___________
Available Goalies:  ___________
Uncertain:          ___________
```

Identify absences and plan F/D player moves:
- Missing forwards? → move someone from D to F
- Missing defense? → move someone from F to D

### Step 2: Build Top Forward Line

**Standard**: select the three highest-rated forwards from `roster.md`.

**Alternative** (if elite players absent): promote next-best rated forwards (skilled tier) to top line.

**Principle**: best three forwards together.

### Step 3: Build Defensive Pairs

Refer to `roster.md` for current D-capable players and ratings.

- **Pair 1 (Shutdown)**: best dedicated D + strong F/D versatile
- **Pair 2 (Two-Way)**: next-best D + F/D not needed on forward lines
- **Pair 3 (Sheltered)**: development D + strong partner (always)

**Critical**: development defensemen always with a strong partner — never two weaker players paired.

### Step 4: Build Second Forward Line

- **Standard**: next three best forwards (skilled tier) not on Line 1
- **Adjust** if a skilled player was pulled to Line 1: promote best middle-tier forward
- **Principle**: two-way responsibility, can play in all situations

### Step 5: Build Third Forward Line

- **Standard**: remaining middle-tier forwards
- **Role**: high work rate, physical, simple play
- **Adjust**: missing one → pull F/D versatile from defense; missing two → consider three-line collapse

### Step 6: Build Fourth Forward Line (Development)

- **Standard**: development tier between two strong middle-tier linemates
- **Principles**: strong linemates, shorter shifts (45-60 sec), offensive zone starts when possible, limited use in critical situations
- **Three-line alternative**: development gets spot duty (5 min) with Line 3 players

### Step 7: Assign Special Teams

Refer to `roster.md` and `team-strategy.md` for current personnel criteria.

- **PP1**: top four skilled forwards + best offensive D
- **PP2**: next-best forwards + strong D
- **PK1**: best two-way forwards + top shutdown D pair
- **PK2**: reliable grind-line forwards + depth D (rotate)

### Step 8: Identify Situational Players

**Late game, protecting lead**:
- Forwards: top two lines only
- Defense: top two pairs
- Avoid: development players

**Late game, need goal**:
- Forwards: top line all hands on deck
- Defense: best offensive D paired with shutdown partner; consider moving elite F/D to D
- Extra attacker: next-best skilled forward

**Overtime (3v3)**:
- Shift 1: two best forwards + top D
- Shift 2: next two + second D
- Shift 3: remaining skilled + third D

## Game Strategy Templates

> These are starting templates. Customize them in `team-strategy.md` based on your team's style.

### vs Skill-Heavy / Star-Driven Teams

**Their typical traits**: speed, high skill, run-and-gun, often weaker D depth

**Tactical focus**:
1. Slow the pace — don't get into a track meet
2. Defensive structure first
3. Disciplined physicality to disrupt rhythm
4. Strong gap control — limit odd-man rushes
5. Keep it low-scoring

### vs Physical / Grinding Teams

**Their typical traits**: big, physical, slower, simple style

**Tactical focus**:
1. Match their physicality — but stay disciplined
2. Outlast them with depth
3. Simple direct play, win battles
4. Strong defensive structure

### vs Weaker Teams

**Their typical traits**: poor record, defensive struggles

**Tactical focus**:
1. Attack consistently
2. Build confidence broadly
3. Get development players extra ice time
4. Work on offensive systems
5. Don't take the opponent lightly

### vs Unknown Opponents (Tournaments)

**Period 1 — Feel them out**: standard four-line, assess style, identify top players

**Period 2 — Adjust**: based on P1 observations, match up appropriately, exploit weaknesses

**Period 3 — Execute**: trust depth advantage, finish strong

**Default**: play your game — your team strategy and identity dictate, not the opponent.

## In-Game Adjustments

### Adjustment Triggers

**Lines**:
- Line getting dominated repeatedly
- Chemistry off (no connection on passes)
- Matchup being exploited
- Injury / fatigue
- Score situation demands different approach

**Pairs**:
- Defense pair beat repeatedly
- Development D overwhelmed (needs stronger partner)
- Need more offense from D (move elite F/D back)
- Need defensive stability (move strong F/D back)

**Special Teams**:
- PP 0-for-3+ (try PP2 or different formation)
- PK struggling (switch personnel, adjust system)
- Specific matchup being exploited

### Common Adjustments

**1. Line shuffle (forwards not clicking)**
- Mix and match within the same tier (interchangeable)
- Keep development with strong linemates
- Keep working lines intact

**2. Move elite F/D to defense**
- When defense struggling OR need more offense from back end
- Slot into Pair 1; promote next-best forward

**3. Three-line system (need top players more)**
- Game close, need best on ice more
- Top line ~45%, Line 2 ~40%, Line 3 ~15%
- Spot duty for remainder

**4. Development D protection**
- If overwhelmed: shorten shifts, upgrade partner
- Or reduce ice time and use other D more

**5. Special teams personnel change**
- Try alternate unit if cold
- Or change formation (umbrella → overload, box+1 → passive)

## Post-Game Analysis

### Immediate (in locker room, 2-3 min)
- Acknowledge effort, highlight positives
- Note 1-2 things to improve
- Look ahead to next game
- Reinforce team identity

### 24 Hours Post-Game

**What worked**: which lines / pairs / special teams / individual performances?

**What didn't work**: which struggled?

**Adjustments for next game**: line changes, pair changes, special teams, tactical approach

**Player development notes**: progress on development players, individual coaching points

## Tournament Strategy

### Multi-Game Weekend Planning

**Pool play (development focus)**:
- Four-line system, balanced ice time, everyone contributes
- Development players: increased ice time
- Both goalies get starts
- Goals: get everyone involved, build confidence, assess what's working

**Playoff games (competitive focus)**:
- Three-line system (or compressed four)
- Top line: 40-45% ice time
- Development players: limited, very sheltered
- Hot goalie gets preference
- Goals: win, best players in critical situations, still everyone contributes

### Back-to-Back Games

| Game | Approach |
|------|----------|
| Game 1 | Normal four-line, full energy |
| Game 2 | Slightly reduce top-line minutes, alternate goalie, manage fatigue |
| Game 3 | Hot goalie starts; may compress to three lines if tired |

## Quick Reference: Decision Trees

### Should I Adjust Lines During the Game?

```
Is a line getting dominated repeatedly?
  → YES: Adjust immediately
  → NO: Continue monitoring

Is chemistry clearly not working?
  → YES: Adjust at next stoppage
  → NO: Give it another period

Are we losing and need a spark?
  → YES: Try line shuffle or three-line collapse
  → NO: Trust the process
```

### Should I Move an F/D Player Mid-Game?

```
Do I have a positional shortage (injury / absence)?
  → YES: Move appropriate F/D player immediately

Is one position group struggling significantly?
  → YES: Consider moving elite F/D to strengthen

Do I need a specific matchup (more offense / defense)?
  → YES: Move player to create the matchup
```

## Final Principles

- **Trust your strategy**: when in doubt, default to your team's identity in `team-strategy.md`
- **Be flexible**: F/D players solve problems
- **Communicate clearly**: tell players why changes are happening
- **Adjust gradually**: small tweaks first, big changes only when needed
