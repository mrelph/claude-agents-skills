# Retirement Rules & Key Numbers

Quick reference for IRS limits, thresholds, and retirement account rules.

## 2025 Contribution Limits

**401(k)/403(b)/457**:
- Standard: $23,500
- Age 50+ catch-up: $7,500 (total $31,000)
- Age 60-63 "super catch-up": $11,250 (total $34,750)

**IRA (Traditional & Roth)**:
- Standard: $7,000
- Age 50+ catch-up: $1,000 (total $8,000)

**HSA (Health Savings Account)**:
- Individual: $4,300
- Family: $8,550
- Age 55+ catch-up: $1,000

## Roth IRA Income Limits (2025)

**Phase-out ranges**:
- Single: $150,000 - $165,000
- Married filing jointly: $236,000 - $246,000
- Backdoor Roth available if over limits (no income restrictions on conversions)

## Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

**Starting age**:
- Born 1951-1959: Age 73
- Born 1960+: Age 75

**RMD calculation**: Account balance / Life Expectancy Factor (IRS Uniform Lifetime Table)

**Key ages**:
- Age 73: 27.4 divisor (3.65% withdrawal)
- Age 75: 24.6 divisor (4.07% withdrawal)
- Age 80: 20.2 divisor (4.95% withdrawal)
- Age 85: 16.0 divisor (6.25% withdrawal)
- Age 90: 12.2 divisor (8.20% withdrawal)

**RMD exceptions**:
- Roth IRAs: No RMDs during owner's lifetime
- Still working: Can delay 401(k) RMDs if <5% owner
- First RMD: Can delay until April 1 of following year (but creates two RMDs in one year)

**Penalties**: 25% of shortfall (reduced from 50% under SECURE 2.0)

## Social Security Full Retirement Age (FRA)

- Born 1943-1954: Age 66
- Born 1955: Age 66 and 2 months
- Born 1956: Age 66 and 4 months
- Born 1957: Age 66 and 6 months
- Born 1958: Age 66 and 8 months
- Born 1959: Age 66 and 10 months
- Born 1960+: Age 67

**Claiming ages**:
- Earliest: Age 62 (70-75% of FRA benefit, depending on birth year)
- Latest: Age 70 (124-132% of FRA benefit)
- Spousal: 50% of higher earner's FRA benefit (if higher than own benefit)

**Earnings test** (if claiming before FRA while working):
- Under FRA: Lose $1 per $2 earned over $23,400 (2025)
- Year reaching FRA: Lose $1 per $3 earned over $62,160 (before birthday month)
- At FRA+: No earnings limit

## Medicare Eligibility & Costs (2025)

**Part A (Hospital)**:
- Eligible: Age 65
- Cost: $0 premium if 40+ work quarters
- Deductible: $1,676 per benefit period

**Part B (Medical)**:
- Standard premium: $185.00/month
- Deductible: $257/year
- Covers: Doctor visits, outpatient care, preventive services

**Part D (Prescription)**:
- Varies by plan
- Average: ~$50/month

**IRMAA (Income-Related Monthly Adjustment Amount)**:

Additional premium surcharge based on MAGI from 2 years prior:

| MAGI (Single) | MAGI (Married) | Part B Premium | Part D Surcharge |
|---------------|----------------|----------------|------------------|
| <$103,000 | <$206,000 | $185.00 | $0 |
| $103-129k | $206-258k | $259.00 | $13.70 |
| $129-161k | $258-322k | $370.00 | $35.50 |
| $161-193k | $322-386k | $480.00 | $57.30 |
| $193-500k | $386-750k | $591.00 | $79.00 |
| >$500k | >$750k | $628.00 | $85.80 |

**IRMAA planning**: Manage income 2 years before Medicare to minimize surcharges

## Tax Brackets (2025)

**Married Filing Jointly**:
- 10%: $0 - $23,850
- 12%: $23,850 - $96,950
- 22%: $96,950 - $206,700
- 24%: $206,700 - $394,600
- 32%: $394,600 - $501,050
- 35%: $501,050 - $751,600
- 37%: $751,600+

**Single**:
- 10%: $0 - $11,925
- 12%: $11,925 - $48,475
- 22%: $48,475 - $103,350
- 24%: $103,350 - $197,300
- 32%: $197,300 - $250,525
- 35%: $250,525 - $626,350
- 37%: $626,350+

**Standard deduction**:
- Single: $15,000
- Married filing jointly: $30,000
- Age 65+: Additional $1,600/person (single) or $1,300/person (married)

## Early Withdrawal Penalties

**Traditional IRA/401(k)**:
- 10% penalty if withdrawn before age 59½
- Plus ordinary income tax on withdrawal

**Exceptions to 10% penalty**:
- Age 55+ separation from service (401k only)
- Substantially equal periodic payments (SEPP/72(t))
- First-time home purchase ($10k lifetime max)
- Qualified higher education expenses
- Medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of AGI
- Health insurance premiums if unemployed

**Roth IRA**:
- Contributions: Withdraw anytime, no tax/penalty
- Conversions: 5-year aging rule before penalty-free
- Earnings: Tax + 10% penalty if under 59½ and account <5 years old

## Key Planning Numbers

**Safe withdrawal rate**: 3-4% of initial portfolio balance (inflation-adjusted)

**Income replacement ratio**: Target 70-80% of pre-retirement income

**Emergency fund**: 12-24 months expenses (longer in retirement)

**Healthcare costs**: $300-400k per couple age 65+ lifetime (Fidelity estimate)

**Long-term care**: Average 3 years for women, 2 years for men (~$60-100k/year for nursing home)

## Important Deadlines

**RMD**: December 31 (April 1 for first RMD)
**IRA contributions**: Tax filing deadline (April 15)
**401(k) contributions**: December 31
**Medicare enrollment**: 7-month window around 65th birthday (avoid late penalties)
**Social Security application**: Up to 4 months in advance of desired start date
