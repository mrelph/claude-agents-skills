## Required Minimum Distribution (RMD) Rules

| Birth Year | RMD Starting Age | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| 1951-1959 | Age 73 | SECURE Act 2.0 (effective 2023) |
| 1960 and later | Age 75 | SECURE Act 2.0 (effective 2033) |

- RMD amounts calculated using the IRS Uniform Lifetime Table
- Failure to take RMD: 25% excise tax (reduced from 50% by SECURE 2.0; 10% if corrected timely)

## Medicare IRMAA Thresholds

Income-Related Monthly Adjustment Amount surcharges apply when Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) exceeds these thresholds (based on tax return from 2 years prior):

| Filing Status | Standard Premium (No Surcharge) | First IRMAA Tier |
|---|---|---|
| Single / Head of Household | MAGI <= $106,000 | MAGI > $106,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | MAGI <= $212,000 | MAGI > $212,000 |

- IRMAA affects Part B and Part D premiums
- Thresholds are indexed and may adjust annually
- Roth conversions and large capital gains can trigger IRMAA surcharges

## Sustainable Withdrawal Rates

| Risk Profile | Withdrawal Rate | Typical Planning Horizon |
|---|---|---|
| Conservative (safe) | 3-4% | 30+ years, high portfolio survival |
| Moderate | 4-5% | 25-30 years, balanced approach |
| Aggressive | 5-6% | 20-25 years, higher income but more risk |

- The "4% rule" originates from the Trinity Study; actual safe rate depends on asset allocation, fees, and market conditions
- Dynamic withdrawal strategies (guardrails, percentage-of-portfolio) can improve outcomes

## Social Security Reference Points

| Item | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Full Retirement Age (FRA) | 66-67 | Depends on birth year; 67 for those born 1960+ |
| Early claiming reduction | ~6.7%/year before FRA | Permanent reduction; up to 30% at age 62 |
| Delayed retirement credit | 8%/year past FRA | Up to age 70; permanent increase |
| Spousal benefit (at FRA) | Up to 50% of higher earner's FRA benefit | Reduced if claimed early |
| Earnings test (under FRA) | $23,400 (2025) | $1 withheld per $2 earned above limit |
| COLA assumption | 2-2.5% annually | Based on historical CPI-W adjustments |
