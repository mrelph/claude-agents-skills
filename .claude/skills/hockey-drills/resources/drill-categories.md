# Common Hockey Drill Categories

This document lists common categories and types of hockey drills to help with searching.

## By Skill Type

### Skating Drills
- Forward skating (stride, speed, acceleration)
- Backward skating
- Crossovers and transitions
- Edge work and agility
- Pivots and tight turns
- Skating with puck

### Puck Handling
- Stickhandling fundamentals
- Dribbling and control
- Toe drags and dekes
- Fakes and feints
- Puck protection
- One-hand stickhandling

### Passing
- Give and go
- Saucer passes
- Backhand passes
- One-touch passes
- Stretch passes
- Cross-ice passes
- Triangle passing
- Breakout passes

### Shooting
- Wrist shots
- Snap shots
- Slap shots
- Backhand shots
- One-timers
- Deflections and tips
- Shooting while skating
- Quick release

### Defensive Play
- Gap control
- Angling and body position
- Stick checking
- Shot blocking
- Defensive zone coverage
- Penalty kill formations
- Backchecking

### Offensive Play
- Cycling
- Creating space
- Screens and deflections
- Power play formations
- Breakaway moves
- 2-on-1, 3-on-2 situations
- Forechecking

### Goalie-Specific
- Positioning and angles
- Lateral movement
- Butterfly saves
- Glove and blocker work
- Rebound control
- Breakaway saves
- Screen shots

### Scrimmages & Games

- Full-ice scrimmages
- Competition drills

## By Age/Skill Level

### Youth/Beginner (U6-U10)
- Basic skating skills
- Simple puck handling
- Fun, game-based learning
- Short attention span activities

### Intermediate (U12-U14)
- Combination drills
- Team concepts introduction
- More complex patterns
- Competition elements

### Advanced (U16-U18)
- Game-speed drills
- Complex systems
- Position-specific work
- High-intensity conditioning

### Adult/Recreational
- Safety-focused
- Skill maintenance
- Modified intensity
- Social elements

## Search Tips

**Be specific**: "U12 passing drills" works better than just "passing"

**Use multiple terms**: "power play one-timer drills" narrows results

**Include level**: "beginner skating" vs "advanced edge work"

**Add context**: "small-area games for practice" vs just "games"

**Try variations**: If "breakout drills" doesn't work, try "zone exit drills"
