## Self-Employment Retirement Options

### SEP-IRA
- Contribution limit: 25% of net self-employment income, up to $69,000

### Solo 401(k)
- Employee contribution: $23,000 ($30,500 if 50+)
- Employer contribution: 25% of compensation
- Total limit: $69,000 ($76,500 if 50+)

### SIMPLE IRA
- Employee contribution: $16,000 ($19,500 if 50+)
- Employer match: Up to 3% of compensation

## Education Credits (2024)

### American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC)
- Maximum credit: $2,500 per student
- 100% of first $2,000 + 25% of next $2,000
- 40% refundable (up to $1,000)
- Available for first 4 years of higher education
- **Income phaseout (Single)**: $80,000 - $90,000
- **Income phaseout (MFJ)**: $160,000 - $180,000

### Lifetime Learning Credit
- Maximum credit: $2,000 per return
- 20% of up to $10,000 expenses
- Not refundable
- **Income phaseout (Single)**: $80,000 - $90,000
- **Income phaseout (MFJ)**: $160,000 - $180,000

## Itemized Deduction Limits

### State and Local Tax (SALT) Deduction
- **Cap**: $10,000 ($5,000 if Married Filing Separately)
- Includes: Property taxes + (income tax OR sales tax)

### Mortgage Interest Deduction
- **Acquisition debt limit**: $750,000 ($375,000 MFS)
- **Home equity debt**: Not deductible (unless used for home improvement)
- **Applies to**: Primary residence + one second home

### Charitable Contributions
- **Cash donations**: Up to 60% of AGI
- **Appreciated property**: Up to 30% of AGI
- **Private foundations**: Up to 30% of AGI (cash), 20% (property)
- **Carryforward**: 5 years for excess contributions

### Medical Expense Deduction
- **Threshold**: Expenses exceeding 7.5% of AGI

## Self-Employment Tax (2024)

### Social Security
- Rate: 12.4% (employer + employee portions)
- Wage base: $168,600
- Maximum tax: $20,906

### Medicare
- Rate: 2.9% (employer + employee portions)
- No wage limit
- Additional 0.9% on earnings over $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (MFJ)

**Self-employment tax calculation**:
1. Net self-employment income x 92.35% = SE tax base
2. SE tax base x 15.3% = SE tax (up to Social Security limit)
3. Deduct half of SE tax above the line

## Foreign Account Reporting

### FBAR (FinCEN 114)
- Required if aggregate foreign accounts exceed $10,000 at any time
- Due: April 15 (automatic extension to October 15)

### FATCA (Form 8938)
- Required if specified foreign assets exceed:
  - Single (US): $50,000 end of year / $75,000 during year
  - MFJ (US): $100,000 end of year / $150,000 during year
  - Single (abroad): $200,000 end of year / $300,000 during year
  - MFJ (abroad): $400,000 end of year / $600,000 during year
