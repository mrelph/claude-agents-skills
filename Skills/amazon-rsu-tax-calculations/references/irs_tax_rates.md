## Capital Gains Tax Rates (2025)

### Long-Term Capital Gains (Held > 1 Year)

| Filing Status | 0% Rate | 15% Rate | 20% Rate |
|---------------|---------|----------|----------|
| Single | $0 - $48,350 | $48,351 - $533,400 | Over $533,400 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $0 - $96,700 | $96,701 - $600,050 | Over $600,050 |
| Married Filing Separately | $0 - $48,350 | $48,351 - $300,000 | Over $300,000 |
| Head of Household | $0 - $64,750 | $64,751 - $566,700 | Over $566,700 |

**Note:** These thresholds are based on taxable income, including the capital gains themselves.

### Short-Term Capital Gains (Held ≤ 1 Year)

Short-term capital gains are taxed as **ordinary income** using the regular tax brackets above.

## Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)

### 3.8% Surtax on Investment Income

Applies to the **lesser of**:
1. Net investment income, OR
2. Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) exceeding threshold

### NIIT Thresholds (Not Inflation-Adjusted)

| Filing Status | MAGI Threshold |
|---------------|----------------|
| Single | $200,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $250,000 |
| Married Filing Separately | $125,000 |
| Head of Household | $200,000 |

### What Counts as Net Investment Income

**Included:**
- Capital gains (short and long-term)
- Dividends
- Interest income
- Rental income (net)
- Royalties
- Passive business income

**NOT Included:**
- Wages and salary
- RSU vesting income (this is wages)
- Self-employment income (active)
- Distributions from retirement accounts (IRAs, 401k)
- Tax-exempt interest

### NIIT Calculation Example

```
Scenario:
- Filing Status: Single
- MAGI: $280,000
- Capital Gains: $50,000
- Other Investment Income: $10,000

Calculation:
1. MAGI over threshold: $280,000 - $200,000 = $80,000
2. Net Investment Income: $50,000 + $10,000 = $60,000
3. NIIT Base: Lesser of $80,000 or $60,000 = $60,000
4. NIIT: $60,000 × 3.8% = $2,280
```

## Additional Medicare Tax

### 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax on Wages

Applies to wages (including RSU vesting income) above threshold.

| Filing Status | Threshold |
|---------------|-----------|
| Single | $200,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $250,000 |
| Married Filing Separately | $125,000 |
| Head of Household | $200,000 |

**Note:** This tax is on wages only, not investment income. RSU vesting income IS subject to this tax because it's treated as wages.

## Social Security and Medicare Tax (FICA)

### 2025 Rates and Limits

| Tax | Employee Rate | Wage Base Limit |
|-----|---------------|-----------------|
| Social Security | 6.2% | $176,100 |
| Medicare | 1.45% | No limit |
| Additional Medicare | 0.9% | Over $200,000/$250,000 |

**RSU Treatment:**
- RSU vesting income IS subject to Social Security and Medicare taxes
- Employer must withhold FICA on RSU income
- If you've already hit the SS wage base from salary, no additional SS tax on RSUs

## Estimated Tax Payment Thresholds

### Safe Harbor Rules

You won't owe a penalty if you paid at least:
1. **90%** of the current year's tax liability, OR
2. **100%** of the prior year's tax liability (110% if AGI > $150,000)

### Due Dates (2025 Tax Year)

| Payment | Due Date |
|---------|----------|
| Q1 | April 15, 2025 |
| Q2 | June 16, 2025 |
| Q3 | September 15, 2025 |

## RSU-Specific Tax Treatment Summary

### At Vesting (Ordinary Income)

| Component | Tax Type | Rate |
|-----------|----------|------|
| FMV of vested shares | Federal Income Tax | Marginal rate (10-37%) |
| FMV of vested shares | Social Security | 6.2% (up to wage base) |
| FMV of vested shares | Medicare | 1.45% + 0.9% if over threshold |
| FMV of vested shares | State Income Tax | Varies by state |

### At Sale (Capital Gains)

| Holding Period | Tax Type | Rate |
|----------------|----------|------|
| ≤ 1 year | Short-term (Ordinary) | Marginal rate (10-37%) |
| > 1 year | Long-term | 0%, 15%, or 20% |
| Any | NIIT (if applicable) | +3.8% |

### Withholding at Vesting

Amazon typically withholds at supplemental wage rates:
- Federal: 22% (or 37% for amounts over $1M)
- Social Security: 6.2% (if under wage base)
- Medicare: 1.45%
- State: Varies

**WARNING:** 22% federal withholding is often insufficient for high earners in higher tax brackets.

### Net Investment Income Tax
```python

    threshold = get_niit_threshold(filing_status)
    if magi <= threshold:
        return 0
    excess = magi - threshold
    niit_base = min(excess, net_investment_income)
    return niit_base * 0.038
```

## State Tax Considerations

State tax treatment varies. Key considerations:

1. **States with No Income Tax:** AK, FL, NV, NH (limited), SD, TN (limited), TX, WA, WY
2. **States with Flat Tax:** CO, IL, IN, KY, MA, MI, NC, PA, UT
3. **States with High Rates:** CA (up to 13.3%), NJ (up to 10.75%), NY (up to 10.9%)

### California Specific (Common for Amazon Employees)

- No preferential rate for long-term capital gains
- All capital gains taxed as ordinary income
- Top rate: 13.3%
- Additional 1% mental health services tax on income over $1M

### Washington State (Amazon HQ)

- No state income tax
- However, 7% tax on capital gains over $270,000 (2025 threshold)
- Exemptions for retirement accounts, real estate, some small business sales
